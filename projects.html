Projects

INTERSHIP AT EMBEDDED SYSTEMS

During my summer internship at the Embedded Systems Lab, my work focused on developing solutions for GNSS-denied environments. I was involved in the assembly and configuration of a small UAV, the Holybro X500 v2, using the ArduPilot flight stack. Additionally, I integrated the PX4FLOW optical flow sensor to enable the UAV to operate effectively in environments where GNSS signals are unavailable. This hands-on experience deepened my understanding of UAV systems and their navigation in challenging conditions.

ELECTRONIS TEAM RAS NTUA

I contributed to the design of the PCB and the programming of the drone’s sensors to ensure proper functionality. My work focused on integrating the sensors with the electronic systems to optimize the drone’s performance for autonomous operations. Additionally, I worked with sensor fusion, particularly implementing the Kalman filter, to enhance the accuracy and reliability of the sensor data for improved system optimization.

OBJECT DETECTION WITH ESP32CAM

I worked on a project using an ESP32-CAM module, where I programmed it with Python, OpenCV, and TensorFlow to achieve real-time car detection, speed estimation, and human detection . By leveraging computer vision techniques and machine learning models, the system could identify vehicles, calculate their speed, and detect pedestrians, making it useful for traffic monitoring and smart surveillance applications.
